01 — Executive Summary

The Big Picture

A comprehensive overview of the project's scope, methodology, findings, and strategic value.

In today's competitive retail environment, businesses rely on data-driven decision-making to improve operational efficiency, increase profitability, and enhance customer satisfaction. Coffee shops generate a significant volume of transactional data every day, making it essential to analyze sales trends, customer purchasing behavior, and product performance. Without proper analysis, valuable business opportunities may remain undiscovered.

This project presents a comprehensive sales analysis of a coffee shop using Microsoft Excel. The analysis was performed on transactional sales data collected over six months, covering January through June. The dataset includes information related to transaction dates and times, store locations, product categories, product types, quantities sold, unit prices, product sizes, and total sales amounts.

The primary objective of this project was to transform raw sales data into meaningful business insights through data cleaning, transformation, Pivot Tables, and an interactive dashboard. Microsoft Excel was used as the sole analytical tool to organize the data, calculate key performance indicators (KPIs), and develop dynamic visualizations that enable users to explore sales performance across different dimensions.

The dashboard reveals several important insights regarding business performance. The coffee shop generated a total sales revenue of $698,812.33 from 149,116 orders, with an average order value of $4.69 and an average of 1.44 items purchased per transaction. Coffee products emerged as the highest revenue-generating category, accounting for 39% of total sales, while Barista Espresso proved to be the highest-performing product type. Sales activity peaked during the morning hours, particularly between 8:00 AM and 10:00 AM, indicating strong demand during breakfast and commuting hours. Among the three store locations, Hell's Kitchen recorded the highest revenue, although all stores demonstrated relatively balanced performance.

The findings of this analysis provide valuable recommendations for business improvement, including optimizing staffing during peak hours, promoting top-performing products, improving data quality, enhancing product bundling strategies, and strengthening customer engagement through targeted promotions.

Overall, this project demonstrates how Microsoft Excel can effectively support business intelligence and decision-making by converting raw transactional data into actionable insights through structured analysis and interactive visualizations.

📌
Executive Takeaway

Coffee accounts for 39% of all revenue. Morning hours (8–10 AM) are the highest-demand window. Hell's Kitchen leads store performance. Barista Espresso is the single highest-revenue product. Together, these four facts define the business's core strategic priorities.

02 — Project Overview

Why This Analysis Matters

Understanding the business context and the strategic value of structured sales analysis.

Business Context

The Retail Data Challenge

The retail food and beverage industry is highly competitive, requiring businesses to continuously monitor their sales performance and customer purchasing behavior. Coffee shops, in particular, serve hundreds or even thousands of customers each day, generating large volumes of transactional data. While this data contains valuable information, it cannot contribute to business growth unless it is properly organized, analyzed, and interpreted.

Sales analysis enables businesses to understand customer preferences, identify popular products, recognize peak business hours, compare store performance, and evaluate overall revenue generation. These insights help management make informed decisions regarding inventory management, staffing, pricing strategies, promotional campaigns, and future business planning.

Project Scope

Excel as a Business Intelligence Platform

With the increasing availability of business data, Microsoft Excel remains one of the most widely used analytical tools due to its powerful capabilities in data cleaning, transformation, visualization, and reporting. Features such as Pivot Tables, Pivot Charts, slicers, formulas, and dashboards allow analysts to summarize complex datasets and present findings in a clear and interactive manner.

This project focuses on analyzing retail sales data from a coffee shop using Microsoft Excel. The objective is not only to summarize historical sales performance but also to identify patterns and trends that can improve operational efficiency and support strategic business decisions.

An interactive dashboard was developed to visualize important performance indicators, allowing users to filter data by month and day while exploring sales across multiple dimensions such as time, product category, store location, and product type.

03 — Problem Statement

The Business Challenge

☕ Turning Transaction Volume into Business Intelligence

Coffee shops process thousands of customer transactions over time, generating large amounts of sales data. Although this information contains valuable insights, manually reviewing thousands of records is both time-consuming and inefficient. Without systematic analysis, it becomes difficult for management to identify customer purchasing patterns, evaluate store performance, monitor sales trends, or determine which products contribute most to business revenue.

The absence of an analytical reporting system can lead to several operational challenges, including ineffective staffing during peak business hours, poor inventory planning, missed sales opportunities, and limited understanding of customer preferences. As a result, business decisions may rely on assumptions rather than factual evidence.

To address these challenges, this project develops an interactive sales analysis dashboard using Microsoft Excel. By cleaning, transforming, and visualizing transactional sales data, the dashboard enables management to monitor key performance indicators, analyze sales across multiple dimensions, and make informed business decisions based on accurate and timely information.

Overall Sales Performance

Figure 1.1 — KPI Summary Overview

During the six-month analysis period, the coffee shop generated total sales revenue of $698,812.33 from 149,116 customer orders. The average order value was $4.69, while customers purchased an average of 1.44 items per transaction.

These metrics indicate a high-volume retail operation where frequent customer visits generate sustainable revenue despite relatively low individual purchase values.

📋 Observation

$698,812.33 total revenue from 149,116 orders over six months — approximately $116,000 per month.

🧠 Interpretation

High-frequency, low-value transactions characterize the business model. Volume is the primary revenue driver.

📈 Business Impact

Strategies that increase average order value — even marginally — would have outsized revenue impact at this transaction volume.

💡 Recommendation

Implement upselling prompts and bundle promotions to lift the average transaction value above $5.00.

13 — Challenges & Limitations

Analytical Boundaries

Recognising the constraints of the analysis is a mark of analytical rigour — not a weakness.

Historical sales data only. The analysis covers January through June. Seasonal patterns, year-over-year comparisons, and long-term trend analysis are not possible with a six-month dataset.

No customer demographic information. Without demographic data, it is not possible to segment customers by age, gender, or preference — limiting the precision of targeting recommendations.

No profit or operating cost analysis. Revenue figures are available, but without cost data, profitability per product, category, or store cannot be calculated — making true margin analysis impossible.

No inventory management data. The analysis cannot assess stock availability, wastage, or supply chain efficiency — important operational dimensions for a food and beverage business.

External factors unavailable. Data on holidays, weather conditions, local events, and competitor activity was not incorporated — all of which can meaningfully influence daily sales volumes.
